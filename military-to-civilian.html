Whether you are a current service member, recently separated, or planning your retirement from the military, you can start preparing now for a career in Project Management. The Project Management Institute and your local PMI Metropolitan St. Louis Chapter are here to help.

Your Military Experience Translates Directly to Project Management

The structured planning and execution you used in service maps directly to the civilian PM lifecycle.

Funding Pathways for PMP Certification

Many members of the military community qualify for programs that cover PMP training and exam costs. Find your situation below.

🪍
Current Service Members
Funding pathways available while you are actively serving
COOL Funding — Credentialing Opportunities On-Line
Learn More: www.cool.osd.mil
  • Available to Army, Navy, Marine Corps, Air Force, and Space Force members
  • Covers certification fees related to your MOS/rating — PMP is approved for many specialties
Act Now Education
Learn More: actnoweducation.org
  • PMP exam prep training for military members, aligned to PMI standards
Onward to Opportunity (O2O) — IVMF at Syracuse University
Learn More: ivmf.syracuse.edu
  • Career development and certification programs available on select military installations
🔄
Transitioning Service Members
Resources available as you prepare to leave active duty
Timing matters. Many programs are most effective when started 6–12 months before your transition date. Check eligibility early.
Act Now Education
Learn More: actnoweducation.org
  • PMP exam prep available for transitioning members; can be combined with SkillBridge
Onward to Opportunity (O2O) — IVMF at Syracuse University
Learn More: ivmf.syracuse.edu
  • Career and certification training designed for transitioning members; covers PMP
⭐
Veterans
Funding options available after separation from service
Act Now Education
Learn More: actnoweducation.org
  • PMP exam prep training available for veterans
Onward to Opportunity (O2O) — IVMF at Syracuse University
Learn More: ivmf.syracuse.edu
  • Covers training costs
  • Covers PMP exam fee for unemployed veterans
Chapter 31 — VA Vocational Rehabilitation & Employment (VR&E)
Learn More: va.gov — Vocational Rehabilitation
  • Covers both training costs and exam fees
  • Available to veterans with a service-connected disability rating
Chapter 33 & Chapter 35 — GI Bill® Licensing & Certification
Learn More: va.gov — GI Bill Licensing & Certification
  • Reimburses PMP exam fees
  • Chapter 33 = Post-9/11 GI Bill®  ·  Chapter 35 = DEA Survivors & Dependents
💜
Military Spouses
Certification funding for current and veteran military spouses
My Career Advancement Account (MyCAA)
Learn More: mycaa.militaryonesource.mil
  • Financial assistance for education and training — strict eligibility requirements apply
Onward to Opportunity (O2O) — IVMF at Syracuse University
Learn More: ivmf.syracuse.edu
  • Covers training costs
  • Covers PMP exam fee for unemployed military spouses
  • Both current and Veteran Military Spouses are eligible
NMFA Military Spouse Scholarship — National Military Family Association
Learn More: militaryfamily.org
  • Scholarship will cover or reimburse training, certification, and renewals
  • One of the broadest coverage options available for military spouses
Chapter 33 & Chapter 35 — GI Bill® Licensing & Certification
Learn More: va.gov — GI Bill Licensing & Certification
  • Reimburses PMP exam fees for eligible dependents and surviving spouses
